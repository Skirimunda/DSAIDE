The Model

Model Overview

This is a simple SIR model with births and deaths and 2 types of direct transmission, density-dependent and frequency-dependent (based on the terminology in (Begon et al. 2002) - see further information tab for references).

This model has the following compartments:

  • S - uninfected and susceptible individuals
  • I - individuals who are infected and infectious.
  • R - recovered/removed individuals.

The processes being modeled are:

  • Susceptible individuals can become infected by two types of direct transmission from infected hosts, further described below.
  • Infected hosts recover after some time (specified by the rate g).
  • Recovered individuals are initially immune to reinfection. They can loose their immunity at rate w and return back to the susceptible compartment.
  • New susceptibles enter (are born) at a fixed rate b. From each compartment (, hosts “leave” after some time (i.e. they die) at rate n. The inverse of this rate is the average lifespan of a host.

The force of infection, \(\lambda\), depends on the transmission model. For density-dependent transmission, it is given by \(\lambda = b_d I / A\), where A is the area under consideration. For frequency-dependent transmission, we have \(\lambda = b_f I / N\), where N is the size of the population, N=S+I+R.

Model Implementation

The flow diagram and the set of ordinary differential equations (ODE) which are used to implement this model are as follows:

Flow diagram for this model.

\[\dot S =b - \lambda S + wR - nS\] \[\dot I = \lambda S - g I -nI\] \[\dot R = g I - wR - nR\]

with \(\lambda = b_d I / A\) for density-dependent transmission and \(\lambda = b_f I / N\) for frequency-dependent transmission.
